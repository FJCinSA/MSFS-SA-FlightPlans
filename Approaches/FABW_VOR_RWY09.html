MISSED APPROACH
Climb straight ahead on runway track 090° to 4500ft, then climbing LEFT turn to 5000ft tracking SLV VOR. Hold at SLV VOR, 090° inbound, right turns. DO NOT TURN RIGHT (south) — Swartberge terrain. Advise Beaufort West Radio of intention.
⚠ PILOT CAUTIONS — BEAUFORT WEST
TERRAIN SOUTH:Swartberge mountains (peaks to 6900ft MSL) lie 20–30nm south. Minimum safe altitude in S sector 7000ft. Do not turn south below 7000ft.
DENSITY ALT:Aerodrome elevation 2929ft in a hot Karoo climate. On summer days ISA+20°C to +25°C, density altitude can exceed 5000ft. Performance calculations critical.
DME RANGE:SLV VOR at ~102nm. DME reception reliable above 6000ft on the approach. Cross-check with SWV (114.40) for position awareness.
Karoo fuel stop: Beaufort West is the natural overnight / fuel stop on the FACT–FALA cross-country, sitting approximately mid-way up the N1 at the gateway to the Great Karoo. Beaufort West is also the birthplace of Christiaan Barnard, who performed the world's first heart transplant in 1967 — fitting context for a CoetzeeMed flight.
